# **Product Discovery Document: AI-Powered Influencer Marketing Marketplace** 

## **Interview Details** 

- **Interviewer:** Ravi (Product Manager) 

- **Interviewee:** Joyee (Influencer Marketing Executive) 

- **Domain:** Influencer Discovery, Campaign Management & Creator Marketplace 

## **1. Background & Industry Insight** 

Influencer marketing is a rapidly growing industry where brands increasingly rely on creators to drive awareness, engagement, and sales. However, the current ecosystem remains highly fragmented and manual. 

Brands struggle to discover the right creators, manage outreach, negotiate deals, and track campaign execution. On the other hand, creators—especially nano and micro influencers—face challenges in discovering brand collaboration opportunities and ensuring timely payments. 

## **2. Current Brand Workflow** 

## **Step 1: Campaign Requirement Gathering** 

Brand managers define campaign requirements such as: 

- Campaign objective (awareness, engagement, sales) 

- Influencer category (lifestyle, fashion, fitness, etc.) 

- Creator tier (nano, micro, macro, mega) 

- Audience demographics 

- Budget and expected deliverables 

## **Step 2: Influencer Discovery** 

Current methods: 

- Google search 

- AI tools such as ChatGPT and Claude 

- Instagram search and similar profile recommendations 

## **Pain Points:** 

- No centralized database of creators. 

1 

- Similar creator suggestions are inconsistent. • Discovery requires significant manual effort. 

## **Step 3: Creator Verification & Analysis** 

Teams manually evaluate creators based on: 

- Content relevance 

- Audience alignment 

- Follower quality 

- Engagement rate 

- Average views and likes 

External tools used: 

- Modash 

- Qoruz 

## **Pain Points:** 

- High follower count does not always indicate genuine influence. • Verification requires multiple external tools. 

## **Step 4: Influencer Management & Outreach** 

Current process: 

- Influencer details and communication status are maintained in spreadsheets. • Outreach happens through Instagram DMs, emails, or agents. 

## **Pain Points:** 

- Manual tracking of hundreds of influencers. 

- Low response rates. 

- Missing contact information. 

- No single source of truth. 

Example: 

A campaign involving outreach to ~140 influencers required nearly one week to complete. 

## **Step 5: Negotiation & Collaboration** 

Once influencers respond: 

- Pricing negotiations take place. 

- Deliverables and timelines are discussed. 

- Collaboration terms are finalized. 

2 

## **Pain Points:** 

- No transparency in pricing. 

- Multiple communication channels create delays. 

- Agreements are difficult to track. 

## **3. Key Problems Identified** 

## **From Brand Perspective** 

- Finding the right influencers is time-consuming. 

- Lack of verified creator data. 

- Managing outreach and negotiations manually. 

- No secure payment and contract management. 

## **From Influencer Perspective** 

- Difficulty getting discovered by brands. 

- Dependence on cold outreach. 

- Uncertainty around payments. 

- Lack of a reliable reputation system. 

## **4. Product Vision** 

## **“Google Ads Manager for Influencer Marketing”** 

A two-sided self-service platform connecting brands and creators. 

## **5. Proposed Platform Workflow** 

## **Brand Side** 

Brands can: 

- Create influencer marketing campaigns. 

- Define requirements: 

- Objective 

- Target audience 

- Creator category 

- Budget 

- Deliverables 

- Timeline 

- Receive AI-powered creator recommendations. 

- Invite creators or review incoming applications. 

3 

## **Influencer Side** 

Influencers can: 

- Create their profiles. 

- Showcase audience and performance metrics. 

- Discover relevant campaigns. 

- Apply to campaigns. 

- Submit pricing expectations. 

- Negotiate collaboration terms. 

A hybrid marketplace approach can be followed where: 

- Brands receive AI recommendations. 

- Influencers can apply to suitable campaigns. 

- Pricing can be negotiated rather than relying only on bidding. 

## **6. Trust & Reputation System** 

A two-way trust score can be introduced. 

## **Influencer Trust Score Factors** 

- Campaign completion rate. 

- On-time delivery. 

- Content quality. 

- Brand ratings. 

- Historical campaign performance. 

## **Brand Trust Score Factors** 

- Payment reliability. 

- Communication quality. 

- Approval turnaround time. 

- Influencer ratings. 

Benefits: 

- Builds transparency. 

- Reduces fraud. 

- Helps both parties choose reliable partners.

## **7. Escrow-Based Payment System** 

## **Problem** 

Brands fear paying before receiving quality content, while influencers fear not getting paid after delivering work. 

## **Solution** 

A platform-managed escrow wallet: 

1. Brand confirms collaboration. 

2. Payment is deposited into an escrow account. 

3. Funds remain locked until agreed conditions are met. 

- Influencer submits deliverables. 

4. 

5. Brand reviews content and requests revisions within predefined limits. 

6. Upon approval, payment is released to the influencer. 

Benefits: 

- Protects both brands and creators. 

- Creates confidence in the marketplace. 

- Reduces payment disputes. 

## **8. Deliverable & Revision Management** 

The platform should define: 

- Number of revisions allowed. 

- Delivery timelines. 

- Approval deadlines. 

- Final acceptance criteria. 

This prevents: 

• Unlimited revision requests from brands. • Influencers refusing agreed changes.
